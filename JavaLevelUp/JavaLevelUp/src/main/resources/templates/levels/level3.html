LEVEL 3

Theoretical part

Most operations in Java are similar to those used in other c-like languages. There are unary operations (performed on one operand), binary operations - on two operands, and ternary operations - performed on three operands. An operand is a variable or value (for example, a number) involved in the operation. Consider all types of operations.

Numbers are involved in arithmetic operations. Java has binary arithmetic operations (performed on two operands) and unary operations (performed on one operand). Binary operations include the following: "+", "-", "*", "/".
When dividing, keep in mind that if two integers are involved in the operation, the result of the division will be rounded to an integer, even if the result is assigned to a float or double variable.

In order for the result to represent a floating-point number, one of the operands must also represent a floating-point number.
There are also two unary arithmetic operations that are performed on a single number: ++ (increment) and -- (decrement). Each of the operations has two varieties: prefix and postfix.

Some operations have a higher priority than others, and therefore are performed at the beginning. Operations in order of decreasing priority:
++ (increment), -- (decrement)
* (multiplication), / (division), % (remainder of division)
+ (addition), - (subtraction)
